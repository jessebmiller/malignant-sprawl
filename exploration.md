# The Exploration Manifesto

What exploration feels like and how we might provoke that feeling in
tabletop games

I'm thinking in pretty general terms here. One could be exploring a 
physical space like a landscape, a neighborhood, or an abandoned cabin,
or one could be exploring more abstract things like a particular part
of history, an academic subject or question, or a personal human
relationship

## Features of Exploration

- Some friction of activity like:
  - hiking or walking when exploring a wilderness or city
  - sorting through old junk in an abandoned cabin
  - interviewing people about their history or knowledge of history 
  - scanning, skimming, reading academic writing on a subject
  - trying on shared values, common ground, dominance, expectations
    to explore a personal relationship

- It's somewhere between wandering and searching
  - It's distinct from wandering in that there is a goal to uncover
    something when exploring
  - It's distinct from searching in that the goal is not so specific
  - The explorer is hiking, sorting, interviewing, scanning,
    negotiating to see what is there and see what can be understood

- The understanding that exploration reveals comes slowly and
  incrementally
  - It is the acceptance and incorporation of whatever happens to be
    uncovered through the friction of activity into an evolving
    understanding
  - The slow and unstructured nature of these revelations allow time
    for speculation, time to wonder

## The Process of Exploration

- Given some unknown arena (wildernes, city, cabin, histiry, subject,
  relationship)
  1. Feel the friction of activity (walking, sorting, interviewing,
     scanning, negotiating)
  1. Recognize facts that present themselves
  1. Integrate what can be integrated of these facts into an evolving
     understanding of the arena
  1. Speculate and wonder about the facts that can't be integrated
  1. Repeat.

## Principles of Exploration

- The arena occupies a stochastic but (mostly) smooth surface with
  the possibility of rare discontinuities (from magic, rifts,
  extreme events etc.)
- This means that adjacent points in an exploration (aka crawl)
  generally introduce small incremental changes along various axes
  like elevation, vegitation, intensity, mood, etc.
- Rarely a large incongruous jump along one or more of the axes will
  be encountered.
- Large jumps to distant points on the surface (teleportation, reading
  a different author, long time away from a person) introduce an
  accumulation of incremental changes leading to larger and more
  random changes along many axes.
- The interestingness of a fact revealed at a given point is drawn
  from a power law distribution
  - As facts increase in rarity their interestingness increases
    exponentially
  - The average interestingness of facts increases as more facts are
    gathered
- Exploration can transition smoothly to searching as an
  understanding and theory solidifies. The work then becomes more and
  more like searching for specific coroborating facts or the answers
  to specific questions

## In a TTRPG

- The speculation of the exploring players can feed the imagination
  and prep of a GM
- I'd expect there to be fruitful play in the differences between a
  GM's speculation and the players speculation
